

# HR Workforce Attrition Analysis Dashboard

Interactive Power BI dashboard for analyzing employee attrition patterns and identifying key factors affecting workforce retention.

---

## Project Overview

Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations because it affects productivity, hiring costs, and overall workforce stability.

This project presents an interactive **HR Workforce Attrition Analysis Dashboard** built using **Power BI** to analyze employee turnover patterns and identify key factors contributing to attrition.

The dashboard helps HR teams and business leaders:

• Understand why employees leave
• Identify patterns across departments, roles, and demographics
• Support data-driven HR decision making
• Improve employee retention strategies

## Key Metrics

Head Count: **1480 employees**

Total Attrition: **238 employees**

Attrition Rate: **16%**

Average Age: **37 years**

Average Service Years: **7.01 years**

Average Salary: **6.50K**

---

## Dashboard Features

1. **Attrition by Job Level** – Distribution of attrition across job levels and departments.

2. **Job Satisfaction Analysis** – Analysis of attrition based on job satisfaction levels.

3. **Attrition by Years at Company** – Shows when employees are most likely to leave.

4. **Attrition by Education** – Attrition distribution by educational background.

5. **Attrition by Age Group** – Highlights which age groups experience the highest attrition.

6. **Attrition by Salary Range** – Demonstrates the impact of salary on employee turnover.

7. **Role-wise Satisfaction of Attrited Employees** – Job satisfaction levels by role.

8. **Gender Distribution** – Attrition breakdown between male and female employees.

---

## Key Insights

• Overall attrition rate is **16%**, with **238 employees leaving out of a workforce of 1480**.

• Employees aged **26–35 show the highest attrition**, indicating early-career professionals are more likely to leave.

• Most attrition occurs in the **salary band up to 5K**, suggesting compensation may influence employee turnover.

• The **Research & Development department records the highest attrition**, followed by Sales.

• Employees from **Life Sciences and Medical education backgrounds** contribute a larger share of attrition.

• **Male employees account for more attrition cases** than female employees.
