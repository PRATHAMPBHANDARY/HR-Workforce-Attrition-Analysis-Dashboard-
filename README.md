# HR-Workforce-Attrition-Analysis-Dashboard-
Interactive Power BI dashboard for analyzing employee attrition patterns and identifying key factors affecting workforce retention.

HR Workforce Attrition Analysis Dashboard

Project Overview
Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations as it affects productivity, hiring costs, and
overall workforce stability. This project presents an interactive HR Workforce Attrition Analysis
Dashboard built using Power BI to analyze employee turnover patterns and identify key factors
contributing to attrition. The dashboard helps HR teams and business leaders understand why
employees leave, detect patterns across demographics, job roles, and departments, and support
data-driven decision-making to improve employee retention.

Key Metrics
1 Head Count: 1480 employees
2 Total Attrition: 238 employees
3 Attrition Rate: 16%
4 Average Age: 37 years
5 Average Service Years: 7.01 years
6 Average Salary: 6.50K


Dashboard Features
1 Attrition by Job Level – Distribution of attrition across job levels and departments.
2 Job Satisfaction Analysis – Analysis of attrition based on job satisfaction levels.
3 Attrition by Years at Company – Shows when employees are most likely to leave.
4 Attrition by Education – Attrition distribution by educational background.
5 Attrition by Age Group – Highlights which age groups experience the highest attrition.
6 Attrition by Salary Range – Demonstrates the impact of salary on employee turnover.
7 Role-wise Satisfaction of Attrited Employees – Job satisfaction levels by role.
8 Gender Distribution – Attrition breakdown between male and female employees.


Key Insights
1 Overall attrition rate is 16%, with 238 employees leaving out of a workforce of 1480.
2 Employees aged 26–35 show the highest attrition, indicating early-career professionals are
more likely to leave.
3 Most attrition occurs in the salary band up to 5K, suggesting compensation may influence
turnover.
4 The Research & Development department records the highest attrition, followed by Sales. 6 Employees from Life Sciences and Medical education backgrounds contribute a larger share of
attrition.
7 Male employees account for more attrition cases than female employees.


Tools & Technologies Used
1 Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard development
2 Power Query – Data cleaning and transformation
3 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Calculated measures and KPIs
4 Excel / CSV Dataset – Data source


How to Use
1 Download the .pbix file from the repository.
2 Open the file in Power BI Desktop.
3 Interact with the dashboard using filters and visuals.
4 Analyze attrition trends across departments, salary ranges, and demographics.


Business Value
1 Identify key drivers of employee attrition.
2 Improve HR decision-making using data insights.
3 Support employee retention strategies.
4 Monitor workforce trends with interactive analytics.
